Government Aid

SSDI

Social Security Disability Insurance (SSDI) or “Disability” provides monthly payments to people who have a disability that stops or limits their ability to work.

Who can get Disability

Individuals may be eligible for Disability if they have:

  • A disability or blindness
  • Enough work history

See if you might be eligible for Disability

What you could get

Your benefits may include a monthly payment and Medicare. Your payment amount is based on your work history before your disability began.

See what you could get from Disability

Try returning to work without losing Disability

You may be able to return to work or continue working without losing your Disability benefits.

Learn about returning to work

What you need to report

Once you’re approved for Disability, you must report changes to your work, income, and personal information.

See what you need to report

Get Disability back if your benefit ended

If your Disability benefit ended, you might be able to restart it later.

Learn how to restart your benefit

SSI

Supplemental Security Income (SSI) provides monthly payments of up to $943 for people with limited income and resources. If you’re eligible, you may be able to get Disability and SSI at the same time.

If you choose not to apply for SSI when you file for Disability, you’ll get a letter saying, “You’re not eligible for SSI.” This won’t affect your Disability application.

Who can get SSI

Adults and children might be eligible for SSI if they have:

  • Little or no income (under $1,971/month for individuals; higher for couples)
  • Little or no resources (under $2,000 for individuals; $3,000 for couples; +$2,000 per child)
  • A disability or are age 65 or older

Medicaid & CHIP

Medicaid and the Children’s Health Insurance Program (CHIP) are joint federal/state programs. Each state agency administers eligibility and enrollment.

Eligibility Requirements

Criteria vary by state and may include household income, family size, age, disability, and immigration status. You must be a U.S. citizen, national, or have satisfactory immigration status.

Visit HealthCare.gov to screen for Medicaid/CHIP eligibility

Waivers

Medicaid waivers can cover caregivers, community transitions, and additional healthcare costs. Check your state’s waiver offerings for details.

Learn more about state waivers

Vocational Rehabilitation (VR)

VR works with people who have physical, intellectual, mental health, or sensory disabilities to gain, maintain, or return to work.

Each state VR agency determines your eligibility and offers services such as:

  • Job training and placement
  • Schooling or tuition support
  • Driver education and vehicle modifications
  • Adaptive technology and assistive software
  • Mileage reimbursement and mobility equipment

Find your state’s VR agency

STABLE Account

A STABLE account lets people with disabilities save and invest for qualified expenses without jeopardizing government benefits.

Are you eligible?

Any U.S. citizen who developed a qualifying disability before age 26 can open an account—or an Authorized Legal Representative can open one.

See if you’re eligible

What are STABLE Account benefits?

Accountholders can save up to $18,000 per year (plus $14,580 more if employed), enjoy tax-free growth, and keep SSI benefits if balance stays below $100,000.

Explore STABLE Account benefits

How does it work?

  • Manage online with easy deposits
  • Contribute up to $18,000/year (plus extra if employed)
  • Choose from five investment options
  • Change investments twice per year

Learn how to set up and manage your account

Qualified expenses

Funds can be used for:

  • Living expenses, education, housing, transportation
  • Employment training and assistive technology
  • Personal support, health & wellness, legal fees
  • Funeral, burial, and more

Department of Developmental Disabilities

The Department of Developmental Disabilities offers Home & Community-Based Services waivers for individuals with disabilities that manifested before age 22.

Waiver services are person-centered and can include in-home support, community integration, and paid caregiving.
